Learn all about Google Glass

by Tiago Bernardo

Updated on 10/March/2023

What is it

The Google Glass is an accessory in the form of glasses that allows users to interact with different contents in augmented reality. Also called Project Glass, the electronic one is capable of taking pictures based on voice commands, sending instant messages and carrying out videoconferences. Its launch is scheduled for 2014, and its price should be US$ 1,500. Currently the Google Glass is in the testing phase and already has a video fully recorded with the device. In addition, the search company filed new anti-theft and screen unlock patents for the accessory.

Release date of

There is no specific and official date for the device to be released, yet. It may be that it will be available in demos from 2013, but its launch for stores is for, at least, 2014.

How it works

According to sources close to Google, the glasses will feature a small LCD or AMOLED screen at the top and in front of the user's eyes. With the use of a camera and GPS, you can locate yourself, as well as select options with the movement of your head.

What You Can Do With Google Glasses

Google's promotional video shows that you can become a kind of "superhuman", since the device can indicate how many meters you are from your destination, if the subway is open or closed, show the weather, schedule and even allow you to arrange meetings with just voice commands.

What's New on Glass

Google finally revealed the complete specifications of Google Glass, and with it a surprise still unpublished in the market: the search giant will use an audio system based on conduction transduction. Through the temples of the glasses, the sound will be transmitted to the user's ear through microvibrations in certain bones of his head, without using any type of speaker.

In addition to the audio surprise, the screen mounted in front of the user's eye also drew attention. There will be 640 x 360 pixels of resolution which, in proportion, would be equivalent to a 25-inch high-definition monitor placed 2.5 meters away from the viewer.
